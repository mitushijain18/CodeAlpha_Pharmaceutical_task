# Pharmacovigilance Research Task

## Project Overview

This project is completed as part of the CodeAlpha Pharmaceutical Internship. The project focuses on understanding the concept of pharmacovigilance, its importance in monitoring drug safety, and its role in protecting patient health.

The main objective of this project is to study how adverse drug reactions are detected, evaluated, reported, and prevented through effective pharmacovigilance systems.

## About Pharmacovigilance

Pharmacovigilance is the science and process of detecting, assessing, understanding, and preventing adverse effects or any other drug-related problems.

It plays an important role in ensuring the safety and effectiveness of medicines after they are approved for public use.

Pharmacovigilance helps healthcare professionals and regulatory authorities identify potential risks associated with medicines and take necessary safety actions.

## Importance of Pharmacovigilance

Pharmacovigilance is important because clinical trials are performed on a limited number of participants before drug approval. Some rare or long-term side effects may only appear after widespread use.

Pharmacovigilance helps to:

- Detect unknown drug risks
- Improve patient safety
- Monitor medicine effectiveness
- Prevent harmful drug reactions
- Support regulatory decisions

## Adverse Drug Reactions (ADRs)

An Adverse Drug Reaction is an unwanted or harmful reaction caused by a medicine when used at normal doses.

Examples include:

- Allergic reactions
- Side effects
- Drug interactions
- Unexpected health problems

## Pharmacovigilance Process

The pharmacovigilance system works through the following steps:

1. Collection of drug safety information
2. Identification of adverse drug reactions
3. Reporting of safety events
4. Evaluation and analysis of collected data
5. Risk assessment
6. Implementation of safety measures

## Sources of Safety Information

Pharmacovigilance data can be collected from:

- Healthcare professionals
- Patients
- Clinical trials
- Medical literature
- Regulatory reports
- Electronic health records

## Role of Regulatory Authorities

Regulatory organizations monitor medicine safety and ensure proper guidelines are followed.

Major organizations include:

### World Health Organization (WHO)

- Provides global guidelines for drug safety monitoring
- Supports international pharmacovigilance programs

### U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA)

- Monitors approved medicines
- Reviews safety reports
- Takes regulatory actions when required

### Central Drugs Standard Control Organization (CDSCO), India

- Regulates medicines in India
- Ensures drug safety and quality standards

## Pharmacovigilance Methods

### 1. Signal Detection

Identifying possible safety concerns from collected drug data.

### 2. Risk Assessment

Evaluating the severity and probability of drug-related risks.

### 3. Risk Management

Developing strategies to reduce potential harm.

### 4. Post-Marketing Surveillance

Monitoring medicines after they are available in the market.

## Applications of Pharmacovigilance

- Large amount of safety data

- Lack of awareness among users
- Maintaining data privacy
